Instructions

This experiment tests how you generalize causal relations.

You will go through two phases of the experiment, a learning phase, and a generalization phrase. All objects in this experiment are governed by the same causal relation(s).

Learning Phase

In the learning phase, you will see six pairs of colored circles subsequently. Each pair looks like this:

Let’s call the colored circle on the left the Agent, and the one on the right the Recipient.

Clicking the play button, Agent will touch the Recipient, and Agent will cause Recipient into a new state - call it the Result. These effects will disappear after 3000ms. We then ask you two questions:

  1. What’s Recipient changed to (i.e what’s the Result)?
  2. What do you think is the causal relation inducing such effects?

Note that all objects in this experiment are governed by the same causal relation(s). You can change your mind at any stage of the experiment, or stick with what you had.

When writing, please use the words Agent, Recipient, Result. Once you clicked the `submit` button, you cannot change what you wrote.

You need to provide at least one, and up to three causal explanations.

Generalization Phase

In the generalization phase, you will be given fifteen pairs of objects - each pair is made of an Agent and a Recipient , and you are asked to make a prediction of the Result.

After you made a prediction, we will tell you whether you are right, or what the right answer is.

You will then be asked to provide the causal relation(s) that cause(s) such effects. Please note that in the generalization phase, while it is still the case that all objects in this experiment are governed by the same causal relation(s), you may see novel features that are not covered by the observations in the learning phase.


When you feel like understanding the tasks, please proceed to the comprehension questions.

Comprehension questions

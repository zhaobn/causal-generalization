Instructions

This experiment tests how you generalize causal relations.

In total, there are 21 trials - takes about 5min to finish. Each trial consists of a pair of objects, as illustrated below.

All the objects in the experiment can be of:

  • Colors: dark red, pale red (pink), dark blue, and pale blue.
  • Stripes: no stripes (plain)
    , left stripes,

Letâ€™s call the colored circle on the left the Agent, and the one on the right the Recipient. When the Agent touches the Recipient, the Recipient will be set into a new state, call it the Result.

Your task is to figure out what causes such effects.

You will first watch one full demonstration, like the one you just played, and try to come up with a causal explanation as good as you can.

Later, you will be given 20 more trials, each consisting of a new pair of objects, and we ask you to make a prediction of what the Result will be given such Agent and such Recipient. All the pairs share the same causal relations as those in the demonstration.

After you make a prediction, we will let you know what the correct answer is, helping you to make a better prediction for the next trial.

During each trial, we ask you to write down what in your opinion is the cause of such effects. When writing, please

  1. Refer to objects as Agent, Recipient, Result.
  2. Refer to object properties as dark/pale, red, blue, plain, left stripes, right stripes.

Once you clicked the `submit` button, you cannot change what you wrote.

You need to provide at least one, and up to three causal explanations.

When you feel like understanding the tasks, please proceed to the comprehension questions.

Instructions

Imagine that you are on an astrological field trip. Space scientists have discovered curious stones with mysterious powers unlike anything seen on Earth! The stones do not all look the same, and how they look seems important for how they interact with one another.

  • The stones discovered so far seem to come in a range of shades including some that are: light, medium, dark, to very dark.
  • And to come in a variety of shapes with different numbers of sides - triangular , square , pentagon , hexagon , etc.

Sometimes the stones go into an active state in which they glow with a strange light . When an Active stone is tapped against another (inactive) stone, the properties of the inactive stone often seem to change.

Any of the mysterious stones can be active or inactive.

You are part of this scientific team to come up with theories about what determines these changes.

Your task will have three phases:

  1. First, you will observe 6 test interactions between Active and Inactive stones.
    You can click the "Play" button to watch the phenomena. After the effects are played, you will then see a summary showing what the Active stone looked like, what the Inactive stone looked like before the Active stone hit it, and again after the Active stone hit it.
  2. Then, you will be asked to write your initial impression of how the phenomena works. What is it about the Active stone and the Inactive stone that makes the changes you observed occur? What changes would you expect for other pairs of Active and Inactive stones that might look different to these ones?
  3. Finally, you will see several new pairs of active and inactive stones and make a guess about what the inactive stone will look like after being hit by the Active stone. You will be paid $0.10 bonus for each guess that you make correctly (according to the true hidden powers of the stones).

While making these predictions, the summaries you saw earlier will be still be shown at the top of the page. Please free feel to scroll up if you need to consult them.

Think carefully. Your predictions are important for science!

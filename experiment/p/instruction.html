Instructions

Scientists just discovered some unusual stones from an expedition. These stones vary in their shapes and color shading.

  • Stones' color shading varies from light, medium, dark, to very dark.
  • And their shapes vary with number of edges - triangular , square , pentagon , hexagon , etc.

These unusual stones are amazing. If an unusual stone touches another, the touched one's properties may change. Let's call the one touching the others the agent, and the one being touched the recipient. Precisely, an agent stone can set color shading and/or shape of an recipient into specific states.

Any of these unusual stones can be an agent or recipient, depending on their role in such interactions.

You are part of this scientific team to discover which properties of the stones cause what effects.

Your tasks contain three phases:

  1. First, you will observe several pairs of stones - agent stones are indicated by having borders, like the illustration below.
    You can click a "Play" button to watch the effects. After the effects are played, you will see a summary of the agent stone, the recipient stone's original state, and the recipient stone's after state.
  2. Then, we ask you to write down your opinions about which properties of the stones cause what effects.
  3. Finally, you will be facing several pairs of new stones, and give your best prediction at the given agent stone's effect on the given recipient stone. You can compose your answer by setting a stone's shape and color shading from two drop-down menus.

While making predictions, the summaries you saw earlier will be shown at the top of the page. Please free feel to scroll up if you need to consult them.

Think carefully. Your predictions are important for science.

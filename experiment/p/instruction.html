Instructions

Scientists just discovered some unusual stones from an expedition. When one of these stones touches another, it may cause the touched stone to change.

Changing stones to certain forms can be very useful for an important research project. Therefore, scientists want to understand how the changes work, i.e., which properties of the stones cause what effects.

What scientists already know about these unusual stones:

  • All the stones are colored by one or two of these colors: dark green, light green, dark orange, light orange.
  • Some stones have stripes - could be north-east pointed
    , or north-west pointed
    ; some are plain
    .
  • The one that touches is the Agent of the change, and the one being touched is the Recipient. Agent and Recipient can be any stones.

You are in this scientific team to determine which properties of the stones cause what effects. We got some samples for your investigation. In these samples, Agent is always on the left, and the Recipient is always on the right.

Firstly, you will observe 9 samples made from different pairs of stones. You can watch the effects by clicking the "Play" button next to each sample.

After observing each sample, please write down your conjecture of which properties of the stones cause what effects. You can use the provided "copy" and "paste" buttons to avoid typing the same thing multiple times.

Then, we will test how good you are by giving you three test samples. In these test samples, you will see pairs of Agents and Recipients, and you need to make a prediction of what the result is by selecting in the panel on the right-hand side of the screen.

After that, you will have a chance to work with some newly-discovered unusual stones - some of them are not included in the previous samples. We ask you to predict what the effects would be according to the causal explanations you came up with. You will get feedback after making each prediction, and you may update your working hypothesis alongside.

If you've fully understood the intructions, please proceed to the comprehension questions.
